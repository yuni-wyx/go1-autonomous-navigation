Why Two Studies

The second study did not replace the first one. It grew out of a limitation in what the first system could represent.

Why start with motion?

  • A robot first needs a motion pipeline that can run online without becoming unstable.
  • That earlier work focused on learned low-level behavior, smoothing, safety signals, and deployment on Go1.
  • Signals such as traversability, obstacle risk, and stability are still important because they describe whether the robot is moving safely enough to be usable.

Why add a second study?

  • Movement alone does not explain what to do when a person is crossing, receding, or hard to interpret.
  • A binary stop/go interface is often too coarse for yielding, side choice, or uncertainty.
  • That is why the later work studies a separate semantic decision layer instead of pretending low-level control solved the whole problem.
