VLM Social Navigation

From motion control to human-aware decisions.

Research question: once the robot can move, how should it decide what to do around people? The answer explored here is an offline decision benchmark using pretrained VLMs, not a fully deployed closed-loop VLM controller.

Research Motivation

The move to VLM evaluation came from a representation bottleneck: binary stop/go behavior was too coarse once the scene depended on people and motion over time.

Why the earlier interface was too limited

  • STOP and FORWARD alone cannot express yielding left, yielding right, or deferring under uncertainty.
  • Crossing direction, receding motion, and late-entering people can collapse into the same low-level response.
  • The limitation was not only control quality. It was the lack of a clearer decision representation.

Why use VLMs here

  • Pretrained VLMs may offer a more semantic reading of person-dependent scenes.
  • The project changes the prompt and output interface rather than training a new controller from scratch.
  • The VLM is treated as a slower reasoning module, not as a low-level actuator.

Decision Representation

The action space is deliberately higher level than motor control. These labels describe what the robot should decide socially, not how each joint should move.

Action Space

  • STOP
  • FORWARD
  • LEFT
  • RIGHT
  • REVIEW

Why REVIEW was added

  • Ambiguous social scenes should not always be forced into a hard binary action.
  • REVIEW makes uncertainty visible instead of hiding it.
  • That does not solve calibration by itself, but it is still a more honest interface.

Benchmark Setup

The capstone is evaluated primarily as an offline decision study over extracted front-camera frames from curated Go1 rosbags.

Data

  • 13 curated Go1 social-navigation bags
  • Decision-level labels rather than low-level control labels
  • Scenario types include crossing, approaching, receding, entering-late, and review-oriented cases

Sequence Setting

  • Sequence window length 10
  • Max frames per VLM call 5
  • Capped temporal subsampling from extracted frame windows

Models

  • Qwen3-VL-30B
  • InternVL-3.5-14B
  • Single-image and sequence-based prompting

Key Results

The numbers are more useful as diagnostic signals than as a claim that the problem is solved.

Benchmark Summary
  • 13 curated social navigation scenarios
  • 0.60 primary bag-level consensus accuracy
  • 0.60 best primary-case accuracy
  • 10-frame window reduced to 5-frame VLM input
How to read this
  • The benchmark shows richer decisions than a binary controller could express.
  • It does not show reliable enough perception to justify raw online VLM control.
  • Unsafe-forward behavior and inconsistent activation remained the main bottlenecks.

Observations

The main value of the run was learning where the new interface helped and where the perception stack still failed to activate reliably.

What improved

  • Sequence reasoning helped in the receding-person case where both sequence methods returned FORWARD correctly.
  • The five-action interface made lateral intent and uncertainty visible instead of collapsing everything into stop/go.
  • REVIEW gave the evaluator a more honest place to put ambiguity.

What remained difficult

  • Crossing and entering-late cases still failed at the consensus level in several bags.
  • Sequence context did not automatically fix ambiguity; sometimes it diluted the person cue that mattered.
  • The interface improved faster than perception reliability did under the current sampling regime.

Deployment Boundary

This phase should be described as an offline social-navigation benchmark and a high-level decision study, not as deployed raw VLM robot control.

WHAT WAS EVALUATED

🧪 Offline

  • Single-image and sequence-based social-navigation policies
  • Decision labels over curated Go1 rosbag scenarios
WHAT WAS NOT DONE

⚠️ Not Deployed

  • No full closed-loop VLM deployment on Go1
  • No raw VLM motor control path
PROPOSED SYSTEM VIEW

✔ Safety-Projected Path

  • Fast controller handles real-time motion and local safety
  • Slower VLM layer provides semantic guidance and review signals

Why the split matters

Raw VLM output is too slow and too unconstrained to trust as low-level control. The more defensible use is a slower semantic layer whose outputs are projected through a safety boundary before anything executable is considered.
